Gilbert, Arizona

Introduction

Incorporated in 1920 and once known as the "Hay Shipping Capitol of the World, Gilbert Arizona has modestly grown to be the fifth largest municipality in Arizona.
Gilbert is home to a population on 288,128 with nearly one third of residents being under the age of 18.
As one of the fastest-rising communities in the United States, the city has become an economically thriving, diverse suburban community.
Home to many companies focusing on biotechnology, clean technology, and renewable energy, Gilbert is set to continue prospering into the future!

Downtown Gilbert by livability.com.

Downtown Gilbert, known to locals as the Heritage District, is nestled in the middle of the city and provides much of the city's well-known eateries and night life.
Whether it be lunch out or drinks at the local country music club, there's always something going on Gilbert Road.

Phoenix Zoo by cronkitenews.azpbs.org.

Opened in 1962 and the largest privately owned, non-profit zoo in the United States, the Phoenix zoo lies just 15 minutes outside of Gilbert.
The zoo has entertained guests and provide homes to hunderds of different animals for over 60 years!
Learn more about the Phoenix Zoo

Tombstone, Arizona by visitarizona.com.

Founded by prospector Ed Schieffelin in 1879, Tombstone became one of the last boomtowns in the American frontier.
The town grew significantly in the late 1800's, seeing its population rise from 100 to over 14,000 in less than 7 years.
Today, Tombstone is best known for its gunfight shows at the O.K. Corral and its host of activities reminiscent of a Western era.
Only a short hour or so out of Gilbert, Tombstone is both a fun and educational experience for all!
Learn more about the Tombstone
